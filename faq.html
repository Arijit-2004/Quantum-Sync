Frequently Asked Questions An Initiative by - Kavach

The purpose of our transaction fraud system is to detect and prevent fraudulent activities within financial transactions. By implementing advanced algorithms and machine learning techniques in Python, we analyze transaction data to identify patterns indicative of potential fraud.

Our fraud detection algorithm employs a combination of supervised and unsupervised machine learning techniques. It learns from historical transaction data, identifying patterns and anomalies that may indicate fraudulent behavior. Features such as transaction amount, location, and frequency are considered to create a robust model.

Python serves as the primary programming language for our project. It is used to implement the fraud detection algorithms, preprocess data, and create a streamlined pipeline for analyzing and flagging potentially fraudulent transactions.

Power BI is employed for data visualization and reporting. It allows us to create interactive dashboards and reports that provide insights into the trends and patterns identified by our fraud detection system. Power BI enhances the interpretability of our findings and facilitates decision-making.

The accuracy of our fraud detection system is measured using standard metrics such as precision, recall, and F1 score. We validate the model's performance by comparing its predictions against known instances of fraud and non-fraud transactions in our dataset.

Our fraud detection system is designed with scalability in mind. It can handle a growing volume of transactions by leveraging distributed computing and efficient algorithms. As transaction volume increases, the system's performance scales accordingly to maintain its effectiveness.
